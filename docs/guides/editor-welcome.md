# Welcome, editors

This site's writing room is a tool called cairn. You don't need an account anywhere, you
don't need to learn anything about how websites work, and nothing you do here can break the
site. Everything anyone writes is kept as ordinary files that belong to your
organization—not to cairn, and not to any company—so your words are yours, permanently. This
page is the two-minute version of everything an editor needs.

## Signing in

Go to your site's `/admin` page and type your email address. A sign-in link arrives in your
inbox; clicking it signs you in on this device. That's the whole system—there's no password
to forget. If the link doesn't arrive within a minute or two, check spam, then ask the person
who runs your site to confirm your address is on the editor list.

## Writing

You write in markdown: plain text, with a few light marks for structure (`#` for a heading,
`**bold**`, a blank line between paragraphs). The toolbar does the marks for you if you'd
rather not type them, and the preview shows your words exactly as the site renders them—the
same styling your readers see, because it literally is the same styling.

The [writing guide](./write-in-the-editor.md) tours the whole surface: headings, links,
images, the insert menu, spellcheck. The [images guide](./add-an-image.md) covers adding and
describing pictures, and the [media library](./manage-the-media-library.md) is where all of a
site's images live.

## Saving and publishing are different things, on purpose

**Save** keeps your work, privately. A saved draft waits for you—readers never see it, and
you can come back tomorrow or next month. **Publish** is the deliberate step that puts your
words on the live site. Until you press it, nothing changes for readers, so you can save
early, save often, and never worry. The [publishing guide](./publish-and-discard.md) covers
the details, including how to discard a draft you've thought better of.

One more piece of quiet safety: if someone else edited the same entry while you were writing,
cairn refuses to save over their work rather than guessing whose words win. You'll never
silently lose anything—yours or theirs.

## When something's odd

Ask the person who runs your site—there's usually a support contact right in the admin's
help screen. Everything you do is kept safe in the site's history, so there is no mistake
here that can't be undone.
