# The five-day arc post-mortem (Fable, 2026-07-06)

The arc: the docs-close through the release (0.80.0), both site rebuilds, the ecxc
production failure and repair, the chassis ontology, three verified theme ports, 0.81.0
shipped end-to-end, the ASC design phase and build, and the phase-2 design suite. Written
on the Fable window's last night; the successor conducts on Opus from tomorrow.

## The central lesson, learned three times at rising cost

**Fidelity fails when verification checks a compression of the original instead of the
original.** 907 lost its typography (the plan said "quite close"; nobody enumerated the
devices). ecxc lost its structure (the plan said "theme layer"; nobody looked at the
site). ASC lost its content surface (the verifiers graded the north star; nobody
enumerated the original's features, images, nav, notifications — the logo itself was
substituted). Each failure produced a stronger countermeasure — the device catalogue,
reference-first capture, finally THE ORIGINAL MANIFEST (2026-07-06): the original
enumerated exhaustively into a committed checklist, every line MATCHED / IMPROVED /
DEFERRED-WITH-SANCTION, verifiers grading the manifest and never the plan. The stance
error underneath: treating the original's choices (a hero, a crop, a logo, a nav item)
as free variables. They are the client's content. The manifest makes that mechanical.

## The verify-the-verifier lineage (now doctrine, each lesson bought with an incident)

Self-graded acceptance fails at every level: builders (the ports' round-1 self-confirms),
fixers (Foxi's false 404 fix), tooling (fullPage screenshots lying on body-scrollers; the
regen artifact echoing its inputs), and the orchestrator (presenting ASC off one page-
read). The working stack: fresh-context verifiers in a while-not-PASS loop; mandatory
numeric probes (contrast on interactive elements, scrollWidth, computed styles) because
screenshots at review scale miss invisible text; the one-check rule; and the owner's eyes
as the final gate — which out-caught the machine all week exactly where checks weren't
yet mechanical. Every human catch became a permanent check the same day.

## What worked and compounds

- **The chassis ontology** (one chassis, N themes) emerged from evidence, was cut in a
  day, and held through four consumers. The removal-note subtractability test and the
  no-reach-ins gate made it real rather than aspirational.
- **The additive contract**: three consecutive release windows, zero Consumers-must
  lines, with the breaking license open the whole time. The contract froze itself by
  being right — beta's strongest credential.
- **The design-sitting method**: evidence exhibits, live candidates, the owner's picks
  iterating in near-real-time (the Season went C0→C7 in an evening, each correction
  making the design truer — mission-first emphasis, link-collision semantics, the color
  story from the club's own materials).
- **The harvest flywheel**: every effort left the stack better (the ledger's LANDED
  column), and per-use iteration became the standing rule for chassis, engine, and
  contract alike.
- **The model economy**: Sonnet moved 5x Fable's volume; the split held under audit
  ($3,545 API-equivalent, 48% of a weekly Fable allowance — the long-context tax the
  correctable leak, session-boundaries the fix).
