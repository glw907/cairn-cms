Media 3a, the inline figure, captions and placement (rev. 2)

The visual contract for the figure render output and the editor controls. A figure is the cairn-reserved :::figure directive wrapping a media image. The placement role rides the class (center, wide, full, plus the bare measure default), and the caption is the directive body text. Tasks 4 through 6 implement against this. Section A is the reader render: the showcase ships these .cairn-place-* values as default CSS and a site overrides them. A season on the early tracks

How four placements read in the flow of a post.

Body text holds the reading measure so the line length stays comfortable. A figure can sit at that same measure, step in narrower, break past it, or run the full width. The placement is the author's call, made once in the control and written into the source as a role class.

Measure default :::figure

The default figure sits at the measure, the same width as the paragraphs around it. It is the right choice for most images in the body of a post.

Center :::figure{.center}

Center keeps a smaller image from stretching to the full measure. It suits a portrait crop or a detail shot, held in the column and centered.

Wide :::figure{.wide}

Wide breaks past the measure to a broader band, still centered on the page. Reach for it when an image earns more room than the text column gives.

Full :::figure{.full}

Full runs edge to edge across the content width. The image goes full-bleed, and the caption steps back to the measure so it stays readable rather than spanning the whole band.
