Media Library, Direction B: the contact-sheet grid plus a live detail panel

Media is visual, so this direction leads with the picture. The main surface is a responsive thumbnail grid, a contact sheet, and a persistent right-hand detail panel updates as a tile is selected, side by side, never a modal. This is the Sveltia and Storyblok model, the strongest library layout in the field study. The grid is a roving-tabindex listbox: each tile has a visible focus ring, a real accessible name, space or enter opens it into the detail, and selection is announced. Thumbnails use object-fit: contain on a quiet mat with the name always in the footer, so a tile never crops the picture to a mystery (the research named cover-crop thumbnails as a real complaint). The headline payoffs ride the detail panel: where-used is exact and cheap, and it gates delete and replace.

1. Light: the grid and the detail panel, a selected tile driving the detail

The top bar carries the Media eyebrow and a live count, search across name, alt, and caption, the filter chips (All, Needs alt, Unused, and a type facet), a grid/list density toggle, and the Upload primary action. The grid holds a realistic set: a needs-alt tile (warning glyph in its footer), an unused tile (the corner marker plus a real label in the detail), and the selected tile, ridgeline-dusk, ringed in violet and driving the panel. The panel shows the large preview, the full metadata, the where-used list of linked entries, the inline alt editor, and Replace and Safe-delete.

2. Multi-select: the selection toolbar replaces the header

Checking a tile enters select mode: every tile shows its checkbox, and a selection toolbar takes the header's place. It carries the count, a Clear, and the bulk actions, here Delete with a usage-aware note that three of the four picked are in use. The checkboxes are real focusable controls, so the selection is reachable and reversible by keyboard. Bulk delete routes through the same usage-aware alertdialog, so a bulk action can never quietly orphan an entry.

6. Narrow viewport: the grid stacks, the detail is a bottom sheet

Below the narrow breakpoint the panel cannot sit beside the grid, so the grid takes the full width at two columns and the detail rises as a bottom sheet when a tile is opened (the dialog-sizing rule). The sheet carries the same preview, status, where-used, alt editor, and actions. Tapping a tile raises it; the grip or the close control lowers it.

Alt text

The bet, in one line. Media is visual, so the picture leads and the management facts ride a panel beside it: the grid is the room, the detail is the desk. Strongest: recognition is instant (contain thumbnails, no mystery crops), where-used and safe-delete sit one selection away, and the side-by-side panel means no modal round-trip to read or act on an asset. Weakest: the panel costs horizontal room, so the grid is denser and the layout leans harder on the narrow-viewport sheet; at a large library the contact sheet also asks more of search and the filter chips than a list would. It leans on the reference principles of a visual-first accessible grid (roving listbox, real names, no cover crops), where-used as the headline that gates delete and replace, safe-delete by usage, a managed load-more over infinite scroll, alt as first-class, and search across name, alt, and caption.
