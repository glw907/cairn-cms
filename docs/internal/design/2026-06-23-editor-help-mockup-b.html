From signing in to live, in four stops

This is the whole path. Each stop opens a short walkthrough. You can leave and come back; the trail remembers where you are.

  1. Sign in by email
    You did this already. We email you a link, you click it, and you are in. No password to keep track of.
  2. Write a post
    Open a post and type. It is plain text with a live preview. The editor shows you how the formatting works as you go.
  3. Save a private draft
    Save keeps your work as a private draft. Nobody sees it on the site until you publish, so you can save as often as you like.
  4. Publish when it is ready
    Publish makes it live on the site. If something is wrong, you can change it and publish again, so it is never the last word.

Screen 4 · Woven contextual help: the point-of-typing hint (light)
cairn's unique opportunity, taught where no major editor teaches it: at the moment of typing. The author just typed ## at the start of a line, so a small, dismissible coach appears beside the caret and explains what it does, with the live result. It is woven, not pushed: it shows only on a first formatting attempt, sits at sibling weight to the line, never blocks typing, and a single dismiss retires it. "More formatting" opens the same cheat sheet the Help view links. The frontmatter-field hint is shown in Screen 5; this screen is the typing one.
