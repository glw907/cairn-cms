The chart had been taped to the inside of the gear closet since before most of the current committee joined, a faded grid of foot lengths against boot sizes that somebody had photocopied from a catalog sometime in the last decade. It was wrong for a meaningful slice of members, especially anyone with a wider forefoot or a narrower heel than the chart's imaginary average foot, and every spring we watched at least two or three people limp through the first outing of the season in boots the chart had told them were correct.

At Trail Days this year, a podiatrist who volunteers with a neighboring club spent twenty minutes with a handful of us and made the case plainly: a length-to-size chart cannot account for volume, and volume is what actually causes blisters. We took the chart down that afternoon. In its place is a short handout on how to check volume and heel lock yourself, plus a standing offer to borrow the club's sizing tools before you buy anything.
