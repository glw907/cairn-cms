Saturday's crossing of the lower loop was, by every account we collected afterward, the muddiest the trail has been in three years. The creek that usually threads quietly under the second bridge had climbed over the planking entirely, and the detour around it turned a dry connector path into a shin-deep bog that swallowed at least one boot outright.

Nobody's camera survived in a usable state. Rosa's bag took on water at the second crossing and by the time we stopped for lunch the phone inside it would not turn on; the two other cameras in the group fared no better once the drizzle turned to real rain around noon. So this report runs with no photos, which is a first for us this season, but the trail itself is not going anywhere and we will send someone back out once it dries enough to make the pictures worth taking.

If you are planning the loop before the next work party clears the drainage, bring gaiters and expect the crossing to add real time to your hike.
