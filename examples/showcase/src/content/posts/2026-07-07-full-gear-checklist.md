New members keep asking for the packing list in full, subcategories and all, rather than the one-page summary we post at the trailhead kiosk. Here it is, nested the way we actually think about it.

1. Clothing
   1. Base layers
      - Tops
        1. Merino wool tee, for cold starts
        2. Synthetic tee, for warm afternoons
      - Bottoms
        - Merino leggings
        - Synthetic boxer-briefs
   2. Insulation
      1. Fleece
         - Midweight quarter-zip
         - Lightweight full-zip
      2. Down
         - 800-fill jacket
         - 650-fill vest, for shoulder season
   3. Shell
      - Rain jacket
      - Rain pants
2. Shelter
   1. Tent
      1. Freestanding
         - 2-person, for most trips
         - 3-person, for family outings
      2. Trekking-pole
         - Ultralight single-wall
   2. Sleep system
      - Bag
        1. 20-degree, three-season
        2. 0-degree, winter
      - Pad
        - Inflatable
        - Closed-cell foam, as backup
3. Kitchen
   1. Stove
      - Canister
      - Liquid fuel, for cold weather
   2. Cookware
      - Pot
      - Long-handled spoon

Members who have been on more than a handful of trips end up owning at least one item from every branch above, which is part of why the closet inventory keeps growing.
