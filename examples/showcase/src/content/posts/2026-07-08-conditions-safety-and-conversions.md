Conditions on the ridge loop have held steady since the last work party, with dry footing above the tree line and only a little residual mud in the lower switchbacks.

::::callout[What changed this week]{tone="note"}
The creek crossing at mile three now has a proper log bridge, so you no longer need to ford it directly. The old ford route is still passable if the bridge is out for maintenance.

:::points
- New log bridge at mile three
- Old ford route kept as a backup
- Blazes repainted from the saddle to the summit
:::
::::

Most of the loop is unremarkable this time of year, which is exactly what you want. The one exception is the section just below the saddle, where last month's storm undercut the trail badly enough that the committee is treating it as a standing hazard until it can be rerouted.

:::alert[Do not shortcut the washout below the saddle]{role=caution}
The trail has slumped nearly two feet along a thirty-meter stretch, and the drop-off below it is not survivable. Stay on the marked detour even though it adds ten minutes; do not attempt the old line, however solid it looks from above.
:::

For members coming from clubs that measure everything in kilometers, the loop is a little over eleven miles round trip.
